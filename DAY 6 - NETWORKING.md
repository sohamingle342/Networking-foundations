## Day 7 & 8: Powerline & Wireless Bridges

### 🧠 Core Concepts Learned 

* **Powerline Networking (Using Wall Outlets):**
  * Instead of running new Ethernet cables through walls, you can use a building's existing electrical wires to send internet data. 
  * You plug one adapter into a wall socket near your router and another near your computer. 
  * *The catch:* High-power appliances (like a vacuum or fridge) can cause electrical noise that slows down the connection. Also, you cannot plug them into surge protectors because those strips block the internet signal.

* **Wireless Bridges (Connecting Two Networks Over the Air):**
  * A wireless bridge is used to connect two completely separate networks (like two different buildings) using Wi-Fi instead of burying an expensive cable underground.
  * To make it work, you configure a wireless device to "Bridge Mode" and manually type in the unique hardware MAC address of the main router so they lock onto each other.
