### 🧠 Core Concepts Learned 
* **What is a VPN?** A Virtual Private Network uses the public internet to create a secure, encrypted "tunnel" between a user and a private network. It allows data to travel safely over public lines by turning readable information into unreadable code (encryption).
* **The Magic of Tunneling:** When you use a VPN, your actual data packets are stuffed inside *another* packet wrapper. To anyone snooping on the network (like an ISP or a hacker at a coffee shop), it looks like you are just communicating with a VPN server, hiding your true destination and data payload.
* **Two Main Types of Corporate VPNs:**
  * **Remote Access VPN (Client-to-Site):** Used by individual remote workers. You install a software client on your laptop, log in, and your machine securely joins the company's internal network as if you were sitting in the office.
  * **Site-to-Site VPN:** Used to link entire physical locations. A hardware router at Branch Office A connects permanently over the internet to a hardware firewall at Headquarters B, seamlessly merging the two office networks without individual users needing software.
